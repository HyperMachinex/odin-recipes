Honey Chicken


Description

This is a Honey Chicken with a crispy coating that`s built to last!
This recipe brings together a plethora of Asian cooking secrets for a Honey Chicken that stays crispy for hours, even after tossing in the honey sauce.
The Chinese frying batter yields a crispy, puffy coating that`s light and not greasy, and requires no special ingredients or equipment!

Ingredients

Chicken & Marinade

  • 300g Chicken Thighs
  • 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Chinese cooking wine
  • 2 teaspoon cornflour/cornstarch

Dredging

  • 1/2 cup cornflour/cornstarch

Stay-Crispy Puffy Batter

  • 6 tablespoon cornflour/cornstarch
  • 4 tablespoon flour
  • 7-8 tablespoon cold soda water
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Oil, For Frying

  • 2-3 cups vegetable oil

Honey Sauce

  • 1/3 cup honey
  • 1.5 tablespoon glucose or corn syrup
  • 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoon chinese cooking wine (or more soy sauce)

Garnish/Serving

  • Sesame Seeds
  • Finely sliced green onions

Steps

Tip For Beginners

  1. Place batter mixing bowl in the fridge with the flour, baking powder and salt. (Helps keep batter cold = crispier chicken)

Marinate & Dust Chicken

  1. Marinade: Mix Chicken and Marinade in a bowl. Refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  2. Dust: Spread the 1/2 cup cornflour/cornstarch on a shallow plate. Scatter over about 8 to 10 chicken pieces, toss to coat, shake off excess, put on a plate. Repeat with all chicken.

Cold Batter & Fry #1

  1. Heat oil: Fill small pot or large saucepan with 4cm / 1.7" oil. Heat to 180째C/350째F on medium high stove (or until chicken starts sizzle straight away when dipped).
  2. Make Cold Batter: Whisk together flour, cornflour/cornstarch, baking powder and salt. Pour in 7 tbsp soda water, then do minimum whisks just to combine (10 or so)
  3. Batter thickness: should fully coat chicken easily, not be see through, but not thick and heavy. Use extra water 1 teaspoon at a time to achieve right thickness.
  4. Dredge: Drop 8 or so pieces of chicken into the batter. Turn to coat, then carefully place in oil.
  5. Fry #1: Cook for 3 minutes until light golden and crispy - when you pick them up, you can tell it's very crispy.
  6. Draing & repeat: Place on rack, repeat with remaining chicken - I cook in 4 batches, don't crowd the pot, brings oil temperature down too much.
  7. Cool chicken for 20 minutes. Meanwhile, make sauce.

Honey Sauce

  1. Place ingredients in a large saucepan over medium heat. Bring to simmer, then leave to simmer for 3 minutes.
  2. Consistency should be like maple syrup. Turn off stove, place lid on to keep warm (when cool, gets too thick to toss chicken).

Fry #2 - For Ultra Crispy

  1. Heat oil to 200째C/390째F.
  2. Add in half the chicken (you can crowd the pot for Fry #2). Cook for 90 seconds until it changes from pale golden to very golden (but not dark golden, chicken will overcook),
    then remove onto rack. More golden = crispier chicken.
  3. Repeat with remaining chicken.

Toss In Sauce & Serve

  1. Tumble into saucepan with sauce, use rubber spatula to quick toss until coated with sauce. (Sauce starts thickening if you take too long, so be quick!)
  2. Scatter with sesame seeds and green onion and serve!
